Data Export Workflow: Houdini to After Effects

Houdini to After Effects Tool

This tool was built to export animation and data from Houdini into Adobe After Effects in a clean and predictable way.

When working with simulations, procedural animation, or technical setups, bringing motion data into compositing tools can quickly become fragile and hard to maintain. This tool provides a structured approach to exporting data and reconstructing it inside After Effects without relying on ad-hoc or manual solutions.

It is designed for technical artists and VFX workflows where Houdini is used for generation and After Effects for compositing or presentation.

Key Technical Solutions

  • Gimbal Lock & Rotation Fix: Features robust quaternion-to-euler conversion logic. Prevents "flipped" cameras or erratic rotation jumps when transferring complex 3D paths.
  • Smart Truncation: Built-in data truncation prevents file size bloat. It ensures that position and orientation data are transferred with high fidelity without the overhead of unnecessary floating-point precision.
  • Multi-Subject Support: Batch export data for multiple cameras and arbitrary point clouds (simulations) in a single pass.
  • Simulation-Driven Accuracy: Specifically designed to handle motion from solvers, VEX-driven procedural animation, and custom Houdini attributes.

What this tool helps with

  • Exporting animation and transformation data from Houdini in a structured and reusable format.
  • Transferring procedural or simulation-driven motion.
  • Keeping Houdini → After Effects pipelines clear and repeatable.
  • Reducing fragile manual setups.

Notes

This is a technical tool intended for users who are comfortable working with Houdini and After Effects pipelines. Some customization may be required depending on the project.

The focus is on clarity, predictability, and maintainability rather than one-click automation. It is designed to fit cleanly into existing pipelines.
